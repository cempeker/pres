A formal treatment

$Y \in \{0,1\}$ is a binary event


$\theta = P(Y = 1)$ latent probability


A decision maker (DM) aggregates judgments from $N$ agents

Each agent $i$ observes

  • shared signal $s \in [0,1] \quad \quad E[s] = \theta$
  • a private signal $t_i \in [0,1] \quad \, E[t_i] = \theta$


$E[\theta | s,t_i] = (1-\omega)s + \omega t_i$


"Linear aggregation problem"
Palley and Soll (2019), Palley and Satopaa(2020)

A Bayesian Model (Palley and Soll, 2019)

$Y \in \{0,1\}$ is a Bernoulli process with prob. $\theta$

common prior on $\theta$: Beta density

$s$: average of $m$ independent realizations of $Y$

$t_i$: average of $\ell$ independent realizations of $Y$

$ \begin{align} E[\theta | s,t_i] &= (1-\omega)s + \omega t_i \\ \omega &= \ell / (m+\ell) \end{align} $

$x_i \in [0,1]$: agent $i$'s prediction

Average prediction:

$\displaystyle \bar{x} = \frac{1}{N} \sum\limits_{i=1}^N x_i $

\begin{align} &= \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N (1-\omega) s + \omega t_i \end{align}

\begin{align} &= (1-\omega) s + \omega \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N t_i \end{align}

\begin{align} \bar{x} &= (1-\omega) s + \omega \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N t_i \end{align}


$ \begin{align} E[\bar{x}|s] &= (1-\omega) s + \omega \theta \neq \theta \quad \text{ for } s \neq \theta \end{align} $


'shared-information bias'
(Palley and Soll, 2019)

The Surprising Overshoot estimator

Each agent $i$ reports

  • prediction $x_i$ on $\theta$
  • meta-prediction $z_i$ on $\bar{x}_{-i}$

Agents report their best estimates \begin{align} x_i &= E[\theta | s,t_i] = (1-\omega) s + \omega t_i \\ z_i &= E[\bar{x}_{-i} | s,t_i] = (1-\omega) s + \omega x_i \end{align}

Agents report their best estimates

\begin{align} x_i &= E[\theta | s,t_i] = (1-\omega) s + \omega t_i \\ z_i &= E[\bar{x}_{-i} | s,t_i] = E\left[ \frac{1}{N-1} \sum_{j \neq i} (1-\omega) s + \omega t_j \middle| s,t_i \right] \\ &= (1-\omega) s + \omega E\left[ \frac{1}{N-1} \sum_{j \neq i} t_j \middle| s,t_i \right] \\ &= (1-\omega) s + \omega x_i \end{align}

Calculate...

\begin{align} p_z &= \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N \mathbb{1}(z_i > \bar{x}) \end{align}

"overshoot rate in meta-predictions"

When does a meta-prediciton overshoot average prediction?


$\displaystyle z_i > \bar{x} $

$\displaystyle (1-\omega) s + \omega x_i > (1-\omega) s + \omega \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=j}^N t_j $

$\displaystyle x_i > \frac{1}{N} \sum_{j=1}^N t_j$ $ = \theta \quad \text{ for } N \to \infty$

\begin{align} z_i > \bar{x} &\iff x_i > \theta \\ \end{align}



$p_z = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N \mathbb{1}(z_i > \bar{x})$ also measures...

the proportion of predictions that overshoot $\mathbf{\theta}$



$p_z = B + C > C$

"overshoot surprise"



$(1-p_z) \%$ quantile

of predictions

gives $\theta$

The Surprising Overshoot algorithm:


  1. Elicit predictions and meta-predictions

  2. Calculate sample overshoot rate $\hat{p}_z$

  3. SO estimator $=$ $(1-\hat{p}_z) \%$ sample quantile


Two factors: Sample size, Sample distribution of predictions

Methodology : A bootstrap approach (Palley and Satopaa, 2020)


  1. For each item, select 1000 bootstrap samples of size $M$.

  2. In each sample, calculate average of absolute errors across the items.

  3. Pairwise differences give 95% bootstrap confidence interval.
